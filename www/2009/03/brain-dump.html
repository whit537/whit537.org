The purpose of this post is to clarify both for myself and for those close to me what I think of myself as "working on." Here is a brain dump. 

I. Village:
  1. I would like to say out loud and first that nothing makes me happier than my children.
  2. About my wife, I would like to say that Jessica and I are each other.
  3. I want to never leave this neighborhood. I want to "tend" it like Wendell Berry wants you to tend a farm. I want to die at 22 Lenzman Ct.
  4. I am a part of (a) the Whitacres, (b) the Lloyds.
  5. I have friends, here and there.
Village Projects:
  • Keep chickens.
  • Don't forget the aitch.
  • Play music.
  • Make the farmer's market.
  • Grow raspberries.
  • Finish our fence/basement/garage.
  • Plant lots of beech trees.
  • Pick up garbage.
  • Start a 3-room schoolhouse.
  • Etc., forever.
II. World:
  1. Participate in metaphysics (Sider, Chalmers, Paul, etc.).
  2. Build Bibligon, so I can read the Bible (Koran, etc.).
  3. Establish XMin? (what is this? who is the audience? TBD, pending I, II.1, & II.2).
  4. Integrate metaphysics work, reading of holy books, and settled ecclesiology (rehabilitation of Kant, metaphysical basis of theology, interpersonalist ethic, etc.).
Assumption, Big: Internet and airplanes mean I can do Village and World.

III. Therefore:
  1. Succeed in business, but only for the sake of enabling Village + World (but still really succeed, somehow).
  2. Succeed in open source, but only because I'm already into it with (a) Aspen and (b) gheat; I want them to succeed and not fail. And I like the people; I miss PyCon. FOSS is tied to business for me. Business means web (for me), and web means Aspen (for me).
That, for what it's worth, is the state of my brain. I believe that 99% of my daily thought life falls within this categorization. Eleven items in three categories, plus Village Projects. If you who know me see me flip-flopping and pursuing something different every day or two, try as an exercise placing my current "whim" somewhere within this taxonomy. I want (of course) to think that my whims fit into a coherent whole.
