I received the following in email, and asked if I could publish my reply here so you can read it, too. :-)

Hi Chad,

I came across your site and saw that you used Lulu to publish Gospels. The resulting book looks to be of very high quality.

My wife and I live in New Zealand. We are interested in self publishing her book of short stories - perhaps 50 pages in length if it were printed on A5 size paper or similar. We would ideally like to publish the book in two languages: English and Hebrew.

I would really appreciate your thoughts on Lulu. This is something neither of us has done before. Hope you don't mind taking the time to answer them.

  • What is the cost to publish?
  • What format would you recommend we use? I was considering using LaTeX to produce a PDF or PostScript file.
  • Is there a minimum print run? I understand Lulu enables you to sell both e-books and printed books.
  • Does Lulu allow you to retain copyright and distribution rights, i.e., can you distribute your book elsewhere?
  • How did you find the overall process?

Thanks for any advice you can give us.

Best regards,
Someone

I did use Lulu for the first versions of The Gospels, yes. The quality was good, though not exceptional (thin paper and no paper choices, glue bindings instead of sewn-through for the hardback). I ended up getting a short run of 100 from a proper book printer, Thomson-Shore, and the cost was 34% lower: $6.19/ea vs. $9.36/ea. at Lulu for paperbacks (hardbacks were $18.86 at Lulu). Lulu does have cost calculators on their site. There's no minimum print run with Lulu, and as I recall the price did not go down significantly with longer print runs. Basically, Lulu works for one-offs, which is a fine place to start.

In terms of software, I found for both Lulu and Thomson-Shore that Adobe Acrobat was a requirement. You're basically on your own if you deliver a PDF produced in any other way than with Acrobat Distiller. Their first trouble-shooting question is, “Did you produce the file with Distiller?” and if you say, “No,” that's what they tell you to do. Again, this can make sense for a one-off: worst-case you're out the ten bucks or whatever. For anything more involved you should expect to invest in or otherwise procure Acrobat. Of course, it's perfectly fine to produce a PDF with LaTeX (I used PrinceXML for The Gospels) and then run it through Distiller before delivering it to the printer.

Lulu as I used them didn't touch on copyright (though the text of The Gospels is public domain anyway). I believe they offer additional distribution services (like cross-listing on Amazon?) that might have further legal implications, but I didn't use them for that. I just used them as an on-demand printer, and as far as that went they were fine.

The other thing I'd say about Lulu is that I have no idea who ordered from me. By doing my own fulfillment (using PayPal to collect payments) I have the chance to interact over email with the people buying from me. The flip side is that I still have 30 books on a shelf a year later, and I'm responsible for shipping them. I bought a case of bubble mailers (I called Uline and asked for samples of a few different options before deciding on one). I address them by hand and mail them at the post office, with a hand-drawn bookmark included. :-)

My biggest piece of advice to you is to go for it! It's a few hours work to upload a PDF into Lulu and order a single copy for yourself for $10-20. Just do it! There will be problems with it but that's a normal part of any process like this. The first crepe is always terrible. The sooner you make those first mistakes the sooner you'll iterate towards a product you're happy with.

Good luck! :-)
