There was a guy a while ago who was on a kick about adding “the game layer” to everything. Dunno where he is now, but he’s kind of right. As life becomes more comfortable, it does seem that goals become more trivial, as if in a game, and less weighty. Disturbingly, we even overtly gamify war now. My goals aren’t survival-level concerns like finding food and shelter, or even really earning a wage.

What are my gamified goals? Some people try to grow the number on their bank account. Jill Kelley chases meaningless honorifics. These days, I find myself more drawn to Facebook likes and comments, Twitter followers and retweets, upvotes and karma on Hacker News, and website pageviews. Michael Brutsch, the troll known as violentacrez, pointed to Reddit karma in his CNN interview as the motivation for his behavior, like a mouse “that pushes the button and gets the food pellet.”

I watched Andrew McAfee’s TEDx talk on how droids are taking all our jobs, including knowledge work. To the extent that he’s right, we will lose our remaining work-related goals. Yet, as gamification suggests, humans seem to want (need?) goals, whether it’s to escape from slavery or finish making an indie video game or just rack up wuffie. Even some sort of spiritual disavowal of goals is ... a goal. It seems that a way to rephrase McAfee’s parting question is, what goals are appropriate in a post-scarcity economy? Maybe it’s Reddit karma. Hopefully it’s not drone kills.

My long-term goals are:

  1. To establish Gittip as a major economic platform
  2. To establish open companies as a significant organizational structure
  3. To articulate abstract.io/n as a nice minimalist philosophy
  4. To articulate XMin as a nice minimalist religion
  5. To enjoy life with my family and friends and neighbors and all of you :-)

I see these goals as suitable for today, and hopefully of ongoing relevance in our continuing shift towards post-scarcity. Certainly they are high up on Maslow’s hierarchy of needs. I expect that they’re also suitably complex and challenging to spend a career on—more satisfying than simply chasing upvotes.

Whence?

Curious about how these goals have developed over time, I just browsed through the previous posts on this blog, and came up with the following related items. I’ll let you draw your own conclusions. For my part I’m encouraged by what I see as a convergence over the past five years. Am I finally finding my place in the world? :-)

  1. What I Am Doing (2012)
  2. Brain Dump (2009)
  3. How to Make Major Life Decisions (2008)
  4. Contentment (2007)

At one point, I remember articulating a life goal of “presenting each other as pure offerings to Yahweh on the Day of Christ.” I’d like to bracket the matter of such supersensible goals for now until abstract.io/n and XMin are further along. Thanks.
