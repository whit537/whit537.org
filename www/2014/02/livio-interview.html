I received the following via support@gittip.com:

Dear Gittip team,

We love projects created for the open. Things that are developed for improvements by everyone. We think opensource is the best way to create. Thats why we made Liv.io - an open platform to discover, follow, support and be part of opensource projects for hardware, software, movies, music, books, places and a lot more to come.

We would like to initiate an interview series about creators of open source and write short stories about their path. Showing ups & downs and the motivation behind the open approach of their project. You are invited to be part and we would love to hear about your story.

[questions]

If you have any material, like pictures, videos or links, you would like us to use please send it!

We’re going to write a short essay about you and your project and’ll show you the result before publishing it on our upcoming blog.

Best regards,

[etc.]

Here are my answers:

Shortly describe what you do!
I run Gittip, a way to give money every week to people and teams you believe in.
Which occasion sparked your inspiration?
I had a great corporate job but still wasn’t happy, and I came to believe that the problem was with the shape of the economy as a whole. I want an economy based on generosity, gratitude, and love. More here.
Tell us more about your motivation that keeps you going!
My deepest motivation is to be found acceptable by Jesus. The Canaanite woman in Matthew 15 is my hero. The Eucharist and playing music and being in the woods and getting drunk and tickling my kids give me energy to return to the Internet and the hustle and the bustle and the noise and to keep going.
What is so special about the opensource approach?
Open-source is special to me because it makes God’s overflowing abundance real and actual and concrete in the world, in history, in human society.
What do you enjoy most when working on your project?
Honestly? I love when I can not answer email or chat or phone for a day and the family is gone and I can just write software all day. That happens rarely. I’m learning to deliver product together with others.
How can I interact with your project?
Check out building.gittip.com.
If you like, tell us about another open project we should ask for an interview!
You should interview Benjamin Lupton from Bevry. That dude loves openness. :-)
