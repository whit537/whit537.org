I received this question in private email:

I’d like a pocket gospels very similar to your layout. How on earth would I go about making that? I think it ought to be about the size of an iPhone with a case, or a moleskine pocket journal. I think 5.5 x 3 would work. Or 5.75 x 2.75.

Let’s work backwards: You want a book in your hand. In order to have a book in your hand, someone has to print the book. In order for someone to print the book, you need to give them a PDF. In order to produce a PDF, you need to use software that can generate PDFs.

I’ve already talked about working with printers in Advice on Lulu. But where does one start to produce a PDF in the first place?

Professional books are usually laid out with Adobe InDesign. If you’re not a designer and/or don’t have Adobe software, you can either hire or otherwise convince a professional designer to lay out your book for you, or you can try to get away with Word or Google Docs.

For The Gospels, I wrote custom software that takes the HTML version of the World English Bible (WEB) and converts it to PDF, utilizing a third-party tool called PrinceXML. I ran the resulting PDF through Adobe Distiller (comes with Acrobat Pro) to make an industrial-strength PDF that a commercial printer can work with.

You get what you pay for. If you search for books on Amazon that happen to be in the public domain, you’ll find plenty of editions with amateur layouts that were probably done in Word. A high-quality book takes an investment of time and money, so you’re either looking at an expensive hobby, or a much bigger conversation about marketing your book and the business of publishing.
